Information We Collect

You have choices about cookies. Most browsers let you refuse or delete cookies, or alert you when a cookie is being set, through their settings menu. You may also be able to control certain analytics or advertising cookies through the tools provided by the relevant vendor. Please note that if you disable some cookies, parts of the Site may not function as intended. Where required by law (for example, in the EU/UK), we will ask for your consent before setting non-essential cookies.

Analytics

We and our service providers may use analytics tools to collect and analyze usage information (such as pages viewed, time on page, and general location) so we can understand engagement and improve the Site. Because our specific analytics providers may change over time, we describe them generically here; in each case, the provider processes information on our behalf and/or under its own privacy policy, and you can typically opt out through the provider's tools or your browser and device settings.

EU / UK (GDPR) Rights

If you are in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights, subject to certain conditions and exceptions:

  • Access — to obtain confirmation of whether we process your personal data and a copy of it;
  • Rectification — to have inaccurate or incomplete data corrected;
  • Erasure — to request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten");
  • Restriction — to request that we limit how we process your data;
  • Portability — to receive certain data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format and, where feasible, have it transmitted to another controller;
  • Objection — to object to processing based on our legitimate interests, and to object at any time to direct marketing; and
  • Withdraw consent — where we rely on your consent, to withdraw it at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

Legal bases for processing. We process your personal data where: (a) you have given consent (for example, non-essential cookies, marketing emails, or the sensitive health/dietary details you choose to share); (b) processing is necessary for our legitimate interests, such as operating and improving the Site and communicating with you, provided those interests are not overridden by your rights; (c) processing is necessary to take steps at your request before or to perform a contract (for example, responding to your inquiry or coordinating your participation); or (d) processing is necessary to comply with a legal obligation. Where we process special-category data (such as health-related information), we do so on the basis of your explicit consent or another lawful basis permitted under Article 9 of the GDPR.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority — in particular, the data protection authority in your country of residence, place of work, or where an alleged infringement occurred (in the UK, the Information Commissioner's Office). We would, however, appreciate the chance to address your concerns first.

California (CCPA / CPRA) Rights

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act, as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act:

  • Right to know — to request the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, the sources, the purposes for collecting it, and the categories of third parties with whom we share it;
  • Right to delete — to request deletion of personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain exceptions;
  • Right to correct — to request correction of inaccurate personal information we maintain about you;
  • Right to opt out of sale or sharing — to opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of personal information. We do not sell your personal information and do not share it for cross-context behavioral advertising, so there is nothing to opt out of; if this ever changes, we will update this Policy and provide the required opt-out mechanism; and
  • Right to limit use of sensitive personal information — to the extent we process any "sensitive personal information," you may direct us to limit its use to what is permitted under the CPRA. We do not use sensitive personal information for purposes beyond providing you the services and accommodations you request.

We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of these rights — for example, by denying you services or providing a different level or quality of service. To exercise your California rights, contact us using the details in Contact Us, and we will verify and respond as required by law.
